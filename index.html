01

The Friction

Human intuition degrades under volatility. Project BWC quantifies that degradation as behavioral drag: the basis-point gap between live discretionary execution and the systematic, regime-aware actions available at the same decision time.

It is an audit of human failure versus systematic logic. The model is not treated as omniscient. It is treated as a fixed counterfactual so execution error can be isolated, reconstructed, and attributed instead of narrated after the fact.

02

The Engine

LAYER 1

Forensic Attribution

Brinson-Fachler attribution paired with an actual-versus-model execution log to decompose allocation error, selection error, timing slippage, and discretionary override cost.

LAYER 2

Probabilistic Measurement

Cornish-Fisher VaR and GBM with Poisson jumps projected to a hard freeze date so forward uncertainty is constrained, not hand-waved.

LAYER 3

Regime-Aware Signaling

A fusion engine conditioned on macro regime, using sparse inverse covariance structure and Hierarchical Risk Parity to project the systematic portfolio the human operator is judged against.
